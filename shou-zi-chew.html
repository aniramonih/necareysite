Shou Zi Chew Bio | Wikipedia

Shou Zi Chew is a popular Singaporean businessman and entrepreneur who has worked as chief executive officer (CEO) of TikTok since 2021. Following graduating from University College London in 2006, Shou worked at Goldman Sachs in London for two years as a banker before he joined Yuri Milner‘s venture capital firm DST Global.

At Russian investment firm DST Global, Shou led investment into JD.com, Alibaba, and Xiaomi. Shou also led a team of early investors in ByteDance in 2013. In 2015, he joined smartphone giant Xiaomi as its chief financial officer. In 2019, Shou became Xiaomi’s foreign business president. In March 2023, he testified before the U.S. Congress concerning the legislative effort to ban TikTok in the United States.

TikTok CEO Shou Zi Chew Congress | Congress | Hearing

In March 2023, Shou faced roughly five hours of questioning by dozens of lawmakers in a high-stakes hearing with the House Energy and Commerce Committee about the safety and security of the immensely popular app. He testified before the U.S. Congress regarding the legislative effort to ban TikTok in the United States. Republicans and Democrats alike grilled Shou about China’s relationship with his company, data privacy, possible teen addiction to the app, misinformation, and fentanyl.

He said TikTok does not promote or remove content at the request of Chinese authorities. Shou also said the app is “free from any manipulation from any government.” His appearance came soon after White House officials told TikTok that it must divest from ByteDance, or face a severe punishment, such as the possibility of a ban.
