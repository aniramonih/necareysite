Who is Meryl Streep?

Meryl Streep, whose full name is Mary Louise Streep, is an American actress. Claiming the title of “the best actress of her generation” frequently, Streep is especially renowned for her ability to change her accent. Over the course of her four-decade career, she has been bestowed with a plethora of honours, including a record 21 Academy Award nominations (of which she won three) and a record 32 Golden Globe Award nominations (of which she won eight).

Meryl Streep biography

Mary Louise Streep was born on June 22, 1949, in Summit, New Jersey to Mary Wilkinson Streep, an artist, and Harry William Streep Jr., a pharmaceutical executive. She has two younger brothers, Harry William Streep III and Dana David Streep, both of whom are actors. Streep’s father was of German and Swiss descent, with his lineage tracing back to Loffenau, from where her great-great-grandfather, Gottfried Streeb, immigrated to the United States.

One of her ancestors served as mayor, and the surname was later changed to “Streep.” Another line of her father’s family was from Giswil. Her mother had English, German, and Irish ancestry, with some of her maternal ancestors living in Pennsylvania and Rhode Island, and being descended from 17th-century English immigrants. Streep’s maternal great-great-grandparents, Manus McFadden and Grace Strain, hailed from the Horn Head district of Dunfanaghy in Ireland.

Streep’s mother, whom she has compared to Dame Judi Dench in both appearance and manner, played a significant role in encouraging and instilling confidence in her daughter from a young age. Despite being naturally more introverted than her mother, Streep would turn to her for guidance and support when she needed a boost of confidence in adulthood.

Streep’s upbringing took place in Basking Ridge, New Jersey, where she was raised as a Presbyterian. She attended Cedar Hill Elementary School and the Oak Street School, which served as a junior high school during that time. Streep’s talent and passion for acting were evident early on, as she made her junior high debut in the play The Family Upstairs, portraying the character of Louise Heller.

In 1963, her family relocated to Bernardsville, New Jersey, where she continued her education at Bernards High School. Despite being described as a “gawky kid with glasses and frizzy hair” by author Karina Longworth, Streep’s inclination to perform in front of the camera was apparent from a young age, as she enjoyed showcasing her talents in family home movies. At the age of 12, Streep’s vocal abilities were recognized when she was chosen to sing at a school recital, leading her to receive opera lessons from Estelle Liebling.
