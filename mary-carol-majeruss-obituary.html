Remembering the Extraordinary Life of Mary Carol Majerus

On December 31, 2023, the world bid farewell to an exceptional individual. Mary Carol Majerus, aged 88, peacefully passed away in Shakopee, Minnesota, leaving behind a legacy that will be remembered for generations to come. Her life was a testament to love, family, and a deep commitment to her community. From her humble beginnings in New Market to her role as a devoted wife and mother, Carol’s journey was filled with cherished memories and meaningful connections. Join us as we celebrate the remarkable life of Mary Carol Majerus.

Life and Family

Carol, affectionately known as Carol, departed this world on December 31, 2023, at the age of 88. Born on May 17, 1935, in New Market, Minnesota, she was the beloved daughter of Joseph and Laura Wermerskirchen. In her early years, Carol met the love of her life, Richard “Butch” Majerus, in fourth grade. Their beautiful journey together led them to exchange vows on August 13, 1955, and they enjoyed 62 remarkable years of marriage. As devoted parents, Carol and Butch raised five daughters and one son, creating countless cherished memories during family outings in their beloved van.

Professional Life and Involvements

Throughout her life, Carol pursued various professional endeavors. She showcased her talents in the bakery at Juba’s Grocery Store, leaving a lasting impression on those who tasted her creations. Later, she ventured into the real estate industry, where she made a significant impact as a dedicated realtor. Carol also contributed her skills as a bank teller before eventually retiring. In addition to her professional achievements, Carol was an active member of the Shakopee Quilt Club and St. Mark’s CCW. Her passion for quilting led her to lead the St. Mark’s Quilting Mission for over two decades, touching the lives of many through her creative and philanthropic efforts.

Predeceased Family Members

Carol experienced the heartache of losing loved ones before her passing. She was preceded in death by her beloved husband, Richard “Butch” Majerus, who held a special place in her heart. Carol also mourned the loss of her parents, Joseph and Laura, her daughter Angela Brewer, her infant son Little Joe, her granddaughters Tiffany Smith and Katherine Marie Brewer-Knight, her great-granddaughter Miakoda Brewer, and her siblings Greg and Jim Wermerskirchen. Additionally, her son-in-law Tor Hylbom left a void in her life.

Surviving Family Members

Despite the profound loss, Carol leaves behind a legacy of love and cherished memories. She is survived by her children Barbara Farrell, Yvonne (Gene) Dodge, Larry (Lori) Majerus, Mary Hylbom, Liz (Chad) Cameron, and son-in-law Cliff Miller. Carol’s siblings Kathy (Bill) Tembrock, Ed (Charlann “Charlie”) Wermerskirchen, and Monica (Richard) Schulzetenberg, as well as sister-in-law Sandi Wermerskirchen, continue to hold her dear in their hearts. Carol’s memory lives on through her 24 grandchildren, 49 great-grandchildren, and five great-great-grandchildren, who will forever cherish the love and wisdom she bestowed upon them.

She leaves behind a legacy of cherished memories and meaningful connections. Throughout her life, Carol pursued various professional endeavors, including working in the bakery at Juba’s Grocery Store and making a lasting impact as a dedicated realtor. Carol was preceded in death by her husband, parents, daughter, son, granddaughters, great-granddaughter, son-in-law, and siblings. She is survived by her children, siblings, grandchildren, great-grandchildren, and great-great-grandchildren. Our thoughts and condolences go out to the family and friends of Carol Majerus during this difficult time.
