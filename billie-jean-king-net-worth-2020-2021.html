Career

In 1972, King’s triumph at the French Open made her only the fifth woman in tennis history to win the singles titles at all four Grand Slam events. She has won a career Grand Slam in mixed doubles. In 1959, she began working with new coaches, including Alice Marble and Frank Brennan, who had won 18 Grand Slam titles as a player herself, as to competing on a huge level. In 1959, she made her debut in Grand Slam at U.S. Championships at the age of 15. There she lost in the first round, and soon she began playing at regional and international tennis championships.

In 1967, King successfully defended her title at the South African Tennis Championships by defeating Maria Bueno in the finals. She played the French Championships for the first time in her career, but there she lost in the quarterfinals to Annette Van of South Africa. One week later, at the Federation Cup in West Germany, King won all four of her matches, including victories over DuPlooy and Helga Niessen Masthoff.
