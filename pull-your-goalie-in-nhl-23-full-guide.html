Pull Your Goalie In NHL 23 Full Guide

 Posted on August 11, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  650 words  |  Kelle Repass

We’ve all played in games where the score was tied or one goal behind and you had to win to save your season. One choice you have in NHL 23 is to remove your goalkeeper from the action and switch him out for another out-player.

This gives you another skater to use in attack while leaving your net vacant, as you might expect. It should only be utilized as a last resort with a little amount of time left on the clock because it is a very risky move that leaves you extremely vulnerable to conceding another goal.

However, it might be a successful strategy to score a consolation goal or win a tie. You can withdraw your goaltender in NHL 23 without pausing the game and stopping an assault if you follow the instructions in this post.

This is How to pull the goalie in NHL 23

Thankfully, pulling a goalkeeper in NHL 23 is rather simple to do with simple controls. On Xbox, you have to simultaneously press LB and View to pull the goaltender. With doing this, the goaltender will leave the goal and be replaced by a different outfield skater.

The controls for Playstation are fairly similar and involve pressing L1 and Share at the same time. Using the same buttons, you can take your additional skater off the ice if you succeed in scoring and put your goalie back in if there is still time left on the clock.

Important Things to Consider When you Pull Your Goalie

When withdrawing your goalkeeper in an NHL 23 game, you need to keep a few things in mind.

If the opposition has a delayed penalty, you do not have to remove your goalkeeper from the rink. When you commit a penalty, your goalie is immediately removed from the game and is temporarily replaced by another skater until the penalty play is over.

Second, even though you have the option to withdraw your goalie at any point during an NHL game, you shouldn’t do so sooner.

As a general guideline, you should only pull your goaltender if you are one or two goals down and when there are two minutes left in the game. It is improbable that you will be able to overcome a three- or four-point disadvantage.

Conclusion

The last option you should use when attempting to score is to pull the goalie. You should only use it when you really must score, not just when you want to score another goal, as doing so could leave you incredibly open to conceding another goal.

It is an excellent weapon to employ in a cup game that you must win since it might just allow you to score the goal you need to force overtime or win the match.

In addition, whether you lose by a score of 2-1 or 3-1 does not matter if you are going to lose and be eliminated from the Stanley Cup. So remove your goaltender from the game and launch one final assault with everything you’ve got!
