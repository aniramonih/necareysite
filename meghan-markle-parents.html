Meghan Markle’s Parents: Thomas Markle Sr. and Doria Ragland

Thomas Markle Sr. and Doria Ragland are the parents of Meghan Markle, the Duchess of Sussex.

They divorced when she was young, and she has stayed close to her mother while her father has become more distant in recent years.

Thomas Markle Sr.

Thomas Markle Sr., born in 1944 in Cleveland, Ohio, boasts an impressive career as an Emmy Award-winning lighting director and director of photography.

He lent his talents to several popular television shows, such as “General Hospital” and “Married… with Children.”

During the 1970s, while both of them were on the set of “General Hospital,” he crossed paths with Doria Ragland.

They tied the knot in 1979 and welcomed their daughter, Meghan, in 1981.

Unfortunately, their marriage came to an end in 1987.

In recent years, Markle has found himself embroiled in controversy.

He faced allegations of orchestrating staged paparazzi photos in the lead-up to Meghan’s wedding to Prince Harry.

Moreover, he has openly expressed his thoughts on his daughter and her relationship with the royal family, sparking public attention.

Doria Ragland

Doria Ragland, born in 1956 in Los Angeles, California, is a social worker and yoga instructor.

Throughout her career, she has collaborated with various organizations, such as the Los Angeles Free Clinic and AIDS Project Los Angeles.

Doria is a trusted confidante of her daughter, Meghan.

Ragland’s strength and resilience have earned her praise.

She raised Meghan as a single mother, providing unwavering support for her daughter’s career and personal life.

A private individual, she has refrained from discussing her daughter’s relationship with the royal family in public.

Relationship between Meghan Markle and her parents

Meghan Markle shares a strong bond with her mother, Doria Ragland.
