Raf Simons Professional Life

Since his arrival in the fashion industry in 1995, Raf Simons has become the epicenter of fashion and one of the most accelerated figures. He accomplished a lot in a few years thanks to his eponymous menswear labor and work as a chief creative officer at Calvin Klein, creative director at Dior, and Jil Sander.

Raf established himself in pop culture over the course of his two decades of career, emphasizing the young blood through the lens of clothing. His creation is flawless, and it unquestionably reflects the profundity of fashion and design.

Raf began his career as an intern for Walter Van Beirendonck while studying industrial and furniture design in Genk.
Van took him to his first fashion show, Martin Margiela’s Spring 1990, at the time. There, he informed The Gentlewoman of the occurrence,

“When I was a student, I thought fashion was a little superficial, all glitz and glamour, but this show changed everything for me. I walked out of it thinking, That’s what I’m going to do. That show is what inspired me to become a fashion designer.”
