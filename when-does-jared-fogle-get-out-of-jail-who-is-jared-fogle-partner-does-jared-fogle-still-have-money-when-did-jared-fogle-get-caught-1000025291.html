When Did Jared Fogle Get Caught?

Jared Fogle was arrested on July 7, 2015, on charges of child pornography and abuse. He pleaded guilty to the charges in November 2015 and was sentenced to 15 years in prison.

Fogle's arrest came after an investigation by the FBI into Russell Taylor, the former executive director of Fogle's foundation, the Jared Foundation. Taylor was arrested in May 2015 on charges of producing and possessing child pornography. As part of their investigation into Taylor, the FBI found that Fogle had received child pornography from Taylor.

Fogle's arrest was a major scandal that tarnished his image as a spokesperson for healthy eating. He had been a popular figure for years, appearing in commercials and ads for Subway and promoting his weight loss story. His arrest and conviction led to Subway ending its relationship with him and the Jared Foundation shutting down.

Who is Jared Fogle?

Jared Scott Fogle is a former American restaurant spokesperson and a convicted sex offender. He gained public prominence through his appearances in Subway advertising campaigns from 2000 until 2015 when he became the subject of an investigation by the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI). This investigation ultimately led to Fogle's conviction on charges related to child sex tourism and possession of child pornography.

During his time as a student at Indiana University, Fogle underwent an impressive weight loss journey, shedding 245 lb (111 kg) between 1998 and 1999. As he had included Subway as part of his diet plan, Fogle's transformation caught the attention of the company, which subsequently hired him as an advertising spokesperson in the following year.

Fogle's popularity grew, and he appeared in over 300 commercials during his fifteen-year association with Subway, as well as making other media appearances.

Allegations of Fogle engaging in inappropriate relationships with minors initially emerged in 2007 but did not gain significant attention until 2015 when the FBI discovered evidence that he had received child pornography from an associate.

Fogle pleaded guilty to charges of child sex tourism and possession of child pornography later that year and was subsequently sentenced to fifteen years and eight months in federal prison. In December 2015, he began serving his sentence at the Federal Correctional Institution in Englewood.
