Pictured: The blonde socialite 'who caused Princeton hedge-fund killer to erupt in jealous rage lead

 Posted on August 25, 2024  |  10 minutes  |  2129 words  |  Patria Henriques

A Princeton graduate who killed his hedge-fund millionaire father flew into rages over a beautiful, blonde socialite in the chaotic months before the shooting, according to new claims.

Tommy Gilbert Jr, 30, is currently being held at Rikers Island on a second-degree murder charge after Thomas Gilbert Sr, 70, was found with a gunshot wound to the head in a staged suicide at his Upper East Side home on January 4.

As the Daily Mail Online first reported in January, Gilbert Jr was also the prime suspect in a fire which destroyed a historic Hamptons home belonging to the investment-banking father of a former friend, Peter Smith.

Now Vanity Fair has revealed that in the months leading up to the murder, Gilbert had attacked Mr Smith, a longtime friend and former roommate, over a misplaced belief that he was interested in Lizzy Fraser.

Gilbert had dated Miss Fraser, a blonde Columbia art history grad with model looks who had also enjoyed a rarefied Manhattan upbringing.

Daily Mail Online reached out to Miss Fraser, who has worked as an assistant at a fine art and antique dealer in Manhattan, for comment.

On Wednesday, a receptionist at her named LinkedIn employer, 1stdibs, in downtown Manhattan, said that Miss Fraser no longer worked there.

Like Gilbert Jr and friends, Miss Fraser, who also attended the elite Barnard College, has been routinely pictured at black-tie opening nights, a host of galas and benefits and book launches.

Mr Smith had no interest in Miss Fraser, friends told Vanity Fair in an article published this week, but it did not quell Gilbert Jr's fury as he accused Smith with Fraser during a vacation in Costa Rica.

Smith, who runs an e-commerce site, was reportedly scared by the behavior of his old classmate -  especially after he had stuck up for him when others had not.

Gilbert 'had been given a pass because of his looks', another acquaintance shared with Vanity Fair, , allowing his odd behavior to often be overlooked.

But those society credentials - educated at $35,000-a-year The Buckley School where he was captain of the football team, and at $50,000-a-year Deerfield Academy in Massachusetts before graduating to the Ivy League - lost meaning in the face of Gilbert's escalation into violence.

Following the nastiness on vacation, when the two men returned to their shared Brooklyn flat, Smith decided he wanted his girlfriend to move in and so asked Gilbert to move out.

The 30-year-old's reaction was to throw more accusations at Mr Smith, accusing him of abusing a recently acquired puppy and then stealing the flagpole from the family's East Hampton home and spearing it through the front door.

In October 2013, Gilbert Jr violently attacked Smith outside of his home in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, leaving him hospitalized.

His former friend filed a restraining order amid reports from friends that Smith was 'terrified' of Gilbert. 

Sources also told Vanity Fair that for all his education and opportunities, Gilbert 'seemed like a guy who was a failure to launch' - failing finance exams and registering companies which came to nothing. 

There was talk of becoming an actor and a model - but likewise came to nothing.

Others reportedly noted that he had mentioned a history of mental health problems and had dabbled in medications.

Last year was dotted with ominous incidents where Gilbert flew into rages. In April 2014, he threatened to kill the golf pro at the exclusive Maidstone club in the Hamptons after he was asked to leave while napping in the men's locker room, Vanity Fair reported. He was barred.

Both the Gilbert and Smith families were members of the elite Maidstone Country Club. The 120-year-old establishment sits in the village of East Hampton and has sweeping ocean views along with a private beach, tennis house and 18-hole golf course.

Former golfers on the links included Jacqueline Kennedy's father, John Vernou 'Black Jack' Bouvier III, and socialite Edith Bouvier Beale, of 'Grey Gardens' fame.

And then on July 5, while watching a gig at a bar in Amagansett, Gilbert assaulted friends Bosco Diaz and Timmy Briggs, trying to choke one man, recalled another friend, James Bohannon in Vanity Fair.  

On September 15, the 19th-century mansion of retired Lazard Freres banker, Peter Smith, was set alight in an arson attack.

Three days later, Gilbert was arrested for violating the restraining order. 

No one has ever been arrested or charged in the arson attack, however, a gas can and gasoline-soaked cloth were found in a nearby cemetery and Southampton police were investigating the case as arson, with the 30-year-old named a person of interest.

Gilbert's girlfriend at the time, former Hell's Kitchen contestant and chef Briana Swanson, later told police that the rags found in the cemetery looked like a set of purple bed sheets that her boyfriend had recently bought, Vanity Fair reported. 

And the first thing multiple acquaintances said of Gilbert Jr when asked to comment by Daily Mail Online, was the rumor that he'd burned down his one-time friend's house last summer.

Additionally a long-time resident of East Hampton and acquaintance of Thomas Gilbert Sr also repeated the allegation.

Peter Smith Jr, 32, and his sister, Bettina Prentice, declined to comment on the allegations.

The home, which was constructed in the 1800s, was completely gutted by the flames overnight on September with fire crews taking several hours to contain the ferocious blaze

The beautiful home was entirely destroyed by the blaze. No one has been charged with the suspected arson although Tommy Gilbert was widely thought to be prime suspect

Thomas Gilbert Jr, 30, being transported from Manhattan Central booking by police for the murder of his father Thomas Gilbert Sr in January

The allegations that Gilbert Jr was behind the arson attack at a fortunately empty home, were being openly discussed among the elite beach community he was once part of, when on January 4, events took a tragic turn.

Towards the end of last year, Gilbert appeared to be isolated from his friends and worried about money after his father threatened to cut his weekly allowance back from $600 to $300 and stop paying his $2,400-a-month Chelsea apartment rent. 

It was widely known among friends and former girlfriends that Tommy had a strained relationship with his father, who had also attended Princeton before going on to Harvard Business School and a 40-year career on Wall Street.

But it appeared that his father's hedge fund, Wainscott Capital, was also not doing as well as he expected. Three years after it was set up, Wainscott was worth just $7.3million - a small amount in a world of billion-dollar funds. The family's summer home in the Hamptons was up for sale.

On the first Sunday afternoon in January, Gilbert Jr visited his parents' $6,000-a-month rental apartment at 20 Beekman Place on 50th Street, a few blocks north of the United Nations on the Upper East Side.

After convincing his mother to leave to buy him a sandwich, he allegedly shot dead his father and positioned the gun in his left hand to make it look like a suicide, before fleeing.

Gilbert Sr's wife Shelley, 67, returned and found her husband dead in the bedroom within minutes of the alleged attack and called 911, police later said. 

Police later found ammunition at Gilbert Jr's Chelsea home after breaking down the door to find him hiding inside. They also found a credit-card skimming device and a stack of blank cards.

Gilbert Jr last appeared in court in February, dressed in an orange Department of Corrections jumpsuit.

The 30-year-old is charged with murder and criminal possession of a weapon along with 21 counts of criminal possession of forgery devices..

Seated with his defense attorney, Alex Spiro from prestigious defense firm Brafman & Associates, Gilbert responded in a deep, level voice: 'Not guilty.' 

Defense attorney Alex Spiro objected to the presence of press photographers in court to Judge Jackson. His request was denied.

Mr Spiro said outside court that his objection was because the murder case is circumstantial without eyewitnesses.

'Because of the garb that they had him wearing, I think it's prejudicial to have his photo published,' the lawyer said. He didn't comment further.

He is next due in court on April 30. It is unclear who is paying his legal fees.

The day after the shooting, a shocked friend told Daily Mail Online: 'He [Tommy] was a really nice person. He was smart, athletic and he went to Princeton.

'He took some leave of absences from Princeton which is why it took him so long to graduate. He must have been really angry to do something so out-of-character.' 

Following the tales from Gilbert's former inner circle, who mostly have chosen to remain anonymous, an ex-girlfriend spoke openly to Daily Mail Online about his dark side in an interview in January.

Manhattan socialite Anna Rothschild, 49, whom the accused killer dated in 2014, said Tommy was a loner with few friends who deeply resented his father and was obsessed with how he would 'never be good enough' for his dad. 

The twice-divorced Upper East Side said he was jobless - and seemed to have few prospects, and little interest, for finding serious work. Instead, he spent most his time in the Hamptons, going to the gym, doing yoga and surfing. 

His one professional aspiration was founding his own hedge fund - but he complained that his father wouldn't give him the seed money to start it, Miss Rothschild said. 

Miss Rothschild said Gilbert Jr. was also preoccupied with how controlling his father was with his money and was convinced that he could never do anything to gain his approval.

Gilbert Jr. has an investment company registered to his apartment - though it's not clear the firm has any assets under investment. The company was called Mameluke Capital - mamelukes being 13th-century Muslim slave soldiers.

The socialite said she was repeatedly warned by her friend that Gilbert 'would chop her into tiny pieces'.

The socialite, who is not related to the powerful Rothschild banking dynasty, exclusively told the Daily Mail Online that when she first began seeing Tommy, after meeting him at a friend's dinner party, a close friend had called her out of deep concern.

Ms Rothschild said: 'Very soon after going out with him, a mutual friend of ours told me: ''Anna you have to be really careful - Tommy is going to chop you up into little pieces.'''
