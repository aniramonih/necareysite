Early Life & Biography

Sergio Ramos García was born on 30 March 1986 in Camas, Spain, to his parents Paqui Ramos and Jose María Ramos. He has a brother, who is currently his football agent. He has a close relationship with his siblings and parents.

His life on and off the pitch has been made into an eight-episode docu-series on Amazon Prime. This was the first time his fans and followers could see the personal side of Ramos and his family.

Personal Life

Ramos met Pilar Rubio, who is a journalist in 2012. They started seeing each other and began a relationship. The couple has three sons together, Sergio Jr, Marco, and Alejandro. They got married in 2019 in Ramos’s home-town of Seville.

Career

Sergio Ramos made his debut in club football in 2004. He appeared in 42 games and helped his team to qualify for the UEFA Cup. The following year, he was selected by Real Madrid to play as center back and scored more than twenty goals in his debut season.

During the 2009–10 season, Ramos was named one of the four captains of Real Madrid. He participated in 33 league matches and scored four goals overall. In 2014, he played the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 win against Sevilla to win their first trophy of the season, the UEFA Super Cup. At the 2014 FIFA Club World Cup, Ramos won the golden ball and was named the player of the tournament.

In 2015, he signed a five-year contract with Real Madrid and was also made the full-time caption of the team. He captained his team to a 10–2 victory over Rayo Vallecano, which was the club’s highest-scoring La Liga victory in 55 years. In 2016, his team won the UEFA Champions League Final, and he lifted his first UEFA Champions trophy as a captain.

In February 2017, Ramos played his 500th match for Real Madrid against Osasuna. In the same season, Real Madrid won their 33rd La Liga title, which was Ramos’s fourth league title as captain.

Ramos made his international debut in 2004 for the Spanish football team at the U19 Euro Cup. He started in most of the matches and helped his team to win the U19 European Championship. He appeared on the senior national team at only 18 years of age, making him the youngest player to play for the national team in the last 55 years. Ramos scored his first international goal against San Marino for the 2006 FIFA World Cup qualifiers.

He was a regular member of the national team at the UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying campaign. Later, Ramos was selected in the squad for the 2009 FIFA Confederations Cup in which Spain finished third. In 2010, he became the captain of the national team for the first time in a match against South Korea. At the 2010 FIFA World Cup, Spain defeated the Netherlands in the finals, and Ramos topped the tournament’s Castrol Performance Index with a score of 9.79.

Awards & Achievements

Sergio Ramos has won several awards in his illustrious career. He won the 2010 FIFA World Cup and the UEFA European Championship in 2008 and 2012, being named to the FIFA World Cup Dream Team in 2010 and the UEFA Euro Team of the Tournament in 2012.

In 2016, he was awarded the Luis Aragones Award and was included in the EA Sports FIFA Team of the Year. Ramos has been named in the IFFHS Men’s World Team thrice, ESM Team of the Year four times, UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season four-times, La Liga Best Defender five times, and many more.
