Early Life & Biography

On February 22, 1955, Shri Thanedar was born in Belgaum, Karnataka, India. He grew up in a low-income family as his father was forced to retire at the age of 55 while Thanedar was only 14 years. Thanedar later worked odd jobs to support his family of eight. He earned a bachelor’s degree in chemistry at the age of 18 years and later attended a master’s program at the University of Bombay.

In 1979 he went to the USA to pursue a PhD at the University of Akron, which he earned in 1982. He became a US citizen in 1988. He did post-doctoral work at the University of Michigan before taking a job in 1984 as a researcher at Petrolite Corp.

Career

Thanedar took job working nights and weekends for $15 per hour at Chemir/ Polytech laboratories to learn the business in 1990. He later took a loan to buy their in 1991 for $75,000 and made sales worth $150,000 in the first year; the business had three employees by then. Chemir’s revenue grew to $16 million by 2005 and had employed 160 people, including 40 PhD. Thanedar borrowed $24 million from Bank of America to finance seven acquisitions offering the bank a personal guarantee to back the debt.

Azopharma, one of his acquisitions, grew rapidly from $1 million in 2003 to $55 million in 2008 and employed 500 people. In 2020 Thanedar briefly retired and later came out of retirement later that year to launch Avomeen Analytical Services, an Arbor-based chemical testing laboratory with his son Neil. In 2015 Avomeen was named to the INC 5000 list first growing companies in the United States of America.

He was named the Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year for the central Midwest region (Missouri, Kansas, Iowa, and Nebraska) in 1999, 2007 and 2016. A buyer of Avomeen Holdings LLC filed a lawsuit in US District Court in Detroit in November 2017, claiming Thanedar made fraudulent and misleading representations of his company’s finances in order to sell the majority stake in November 2016.

However, Thanedar denied the allegations. Thanedar entered politics when he chose to run in the 2018 Michigan gubernatorial election as a democrat. His political platform includes a $15 minimum wage, public education reform, infrastructure improvements increased government transparency.
