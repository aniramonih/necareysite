The Inspiration for You Should Be Here

Cole wrote this song as a tribute to his father who died unexpectedly in a tragic accident. The lyrics reflect his personal experience of losing someone important and how it feels to miss them deeply. Cole’s father was his biggest inspiration and supporter, and his absence was deeply felt. Cole wrote the song as a way to express his feelings of loss and to honor his father’s memory.

The Lyrics of You Should Be Here

The lyrics of “You Should Be Here” capture the feeling of missing someone deeply. The first verse of the song starts with “It’s perfect outside, it’s like God let me dial up the weather”, and goes on to describe how beautiful the day is. This sets up the contrast between the perfect day and the feeling of emptiness that is felt at the absence of a loved one. The chorus is the most emotional part of the song and is meant to convey the desire to be with someone who is no longer here. The lyrics say:

“‘Cause there’s a bigger picture That I’m looking at, on my own And missing you comes in waves And tonight I’m drowning”

The lyrics of the song are very relatable because everyone has lost someone they love at some point in their lives. The way Cole has expressed his feelings of loss and longing strikes a chord with listeners, and many have been moved to tears by the song.

The Impact of You Should Be Here

The song has had a huge impact on the audience, and it has become an anthem for remembering loved ones who have passed away. It has been streamed over 400 million times on Spotify, and the video has over 90 million views on YouTube. The song has also won several awards, including Song of the Year at the 2016 BMI Country Awards.

The song has become so popular that Cole has started a campaign called “You Should Be Here” to raise money for cancer research. The campaign encourages people to share their own stories of loss and to donate to cancer research in memory of their loved ones.

The Future of You Should Be Here

Although the song is several years old, its impact remains strong. Cole continues to perform the song at his concerts, and it always receives a great response from the audience. The song has become a classic in the country music genre, and it will continue to be a favorite of many people for years to come.
