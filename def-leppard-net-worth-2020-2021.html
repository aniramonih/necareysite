Def Leppard is a famous rock band in Britain. It is one of the key drivers of the British heavy metal in the 1980s. In 1992, the band released a hit album, ‘Adrenalize,’ that entered the American charts at the top position and stood there for five consecutive weeks. Its first single as ‘Let’s Get Rocked,’ remained on MTV’s countdown for several weeks. It got nominated for “Best Video of the Year” at the ninth MTV Video Music Awards in 1992. Def Leppard is one of the most significant forces in rock music, with over 65 million albums sold throughout the world and two valuable Diamond Awards to its credit.

Inception

The Def Leppard rock band was formed in 1977 in Sheffield, England. The band was the part of the British Heavy Metal. Some of the original band members include Pete Willis(guitars), Rick Savage (bass), Joe Elliott (vocalist), and Tony Kenning(drums). Earlier the name of the band was kept as “Atomic Mass” by the original band members, later the name was changed to Def Leppard by Joe Elliott.

Initially, the band started off playing locally, and after some time, a few more members were included in this group as Steve Clark, Phil Collen, Rick Allen, and Vivian Campbell. The band got an opportunity to work with Mercury Records and signed a deal with this recording studio.

Awards & Achievements

The band defined and established their style through the hit album, High ‘n’ Dry (1981). Their main track of the album “Bringin’ On the Heartbreak” was one of their first rock videos that got played in 1982 on MTV. The next studio album of the band as “Pyromania” (1983), comprised of lead singles as “Rock of Ages,” and “Photograph.” This album got diamond certification and made it a highly popular music band of that time. In 2003, this album stood at 384 positions on the list of 500 Greatest Albums of All Time by Rolling Stone.

Their studio album, Adrenalize, made its way to the No. 1 position in the US and UK charts in 1992. It also contained various hits that included “Have You Ever Needed Someone So Bad,” and “Let’s Get Rocked.” In 1995, their greatest-hits music album, Vault, was released, and it featured the British hit “When Love & Hate Collide.” Def Leppard band won awards at the 1989 US Music Awards for favorite Hard Rock/ Heavy Metal Artist and music album for Hysteria.

Due to this significant contribution in the field of the music industry, Def Leppard made its place in the “Rock and Roll Hall of Fame” in the year 2019. With classic hits such as “Rock of Ages,” “Pour Some Sugar on Me,” “Hysteria,” “Foolin,” “Photograph,” and exceptional version of “Bringing on the Heartache,” the band continues to connect its powerful energy and rock sound with the strength of emotional essence of their musical creation.
