Introduction

Aaron Ross is a former American football cornerback who has played for the Baltimore Ravens, the Jacksonville Jaguars, and the New York Giants.

Ross won Super Bowl XLII and Super Bowl XLVI with the New York Giants.

Biography

He was born on September 15, 1982, in San Antonio, Texas.

Ross only started to play football in high school. 

“My mom had us at a young age, so my grandmother raised us,” Aaron said in April 2013. “She really didn’t allow us to play sports. I kind of moved away with my dad and moved to San Antonio. I started school there and then got into a little bit of trouble so I had to go back into East Texas, and that’s what kind of settled me in; the coach told me, set me down, and told me that I really had an opportunity to become a star.”

While attending John Tyler High School, Ross played as a running back, wide receiver, and quarterback, under coach Allen Wilson — who won two Texas state championships.

@Getty

Aaron also played at Fox Tech High School.

During college, Ross played for the Texas Longhorns football team. 

“The Longhorns, it’s like a lifelong dream to play to become a Longhorn, so once you UT coach came to talk to me, I was sold,” Ross said in an interview with The New York Giants in September 2017.

“They offered me a scholarship. I didn’t take another visit anywhere else. Coming to a place that you’ve always dreamt about coming to and having a chance to be a part of a great team, it was like a brotherhood. We were trained together, we eat together, we party together, we would do everything together.”

In 2005, Aaron won the National Championship.

In 2006, Ross was the recipient of the Jim Thorpe Award.

NFL

@Getty

In July 2007, he was drafted by the New York Giants with a five-year, $13 million contract.

Ross was one of seven University of Texas players to be selected in the 2007 NFL Draft.

“From day one, I wanted to be out there with the vets, with the rookies and the coaches, everybody,” he revealed at the time.

In his rookie season, he recorded 42 tackles.

In March 2012, he was signed by The Jacksonville Jaguars on a three-year, $15 million contract.

“I loved the opportunity that they gave me, they gave me a second chance to live my dream,” Ross said.

Ross was released on March 8, 2013. He started nine of 14 games with the Jaguars.

On March 12, 2013, Aaron inked a deal with the New York Giants on a one-year, $715,000 contract, plus a $65,000 signing bonus.

In June 2014, The Ravens signed Aaron to compete for a reserve role. The team also signed Nick DiMarco, Austin Spitler, and Dominique Franks.

In July 2014, he suffered an Achilles tendon tear during the team’s conditioning test.

On August 16, 2015, Aarong signed with the Cleveland Browns. He was released in the same month.

Wife

In 2010, Aaron Ross married Sanya Richards-Ross (née Richards) after seven years of dating.

@Getty

The wedding was featured on the TV show “Platinum Weddings.”

“She was there for both of my Super Bowls — that’s the top point of my life,” Ross confessed in an interview with Yahoo. “We met in our freshman year.”

Note – Sanya is a retired Jamaican-American track and field athlete. At age 12, she moved from Jamaica to Florida with her family. In 2006, Sanya set the American record for 400m (48.70). She announced her retirement in 2016.

“It’s her birthday, but she’s been my gift for the last 16 years,” Aaron said in February 2019 about his wife.

Children

In August 2017, Aaron and Sanya welcomed their first child, a son named Aaron Jermaine Ross II.

“We are so excited to start a family and can’t wait to begin this new adventure,” the two said in 2017.

“I love when he tells you, ‘mommy I will always take care of you,'” Sanya wrote on her Instagram in May 2021.
