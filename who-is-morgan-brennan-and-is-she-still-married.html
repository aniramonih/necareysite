Morgan Brennan Started Practicing Journalism in 2009

Morgan Brennan’s Journalism career kicked off just after she graduated from New York University. She landed her first job two days after graduating from college at Forbes Media as an assistant in the video department.

She subsequently started working as a staff writer and reporter, and throughout her time at Forbes, Morgan worked on several projects, including Forbes.com and ForbesLife Magazine.

She also reported on and published issues relating to finance, real estate, business, economics, etc. Morgan left Forbes and briefly worked as an economics journalist at Newsweek International before joining CNBC.

Her CNBC Career

Morgan Brennan joined the CNBC news channel in December 2013. She started working as a general assignment reporter based at CNBC Global Headquarters in Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey. At first, she worked for CNBC’s show Squawk Alley, reporting on various issues such as defense, space, and manufacturing.

Brennan also contributes to business and finance news for various other CNBC shows. On 22nd June 2020, she was made the co-anchor of Squawk on the Street, a position she has held to date. She replaced Sara Eisen. Squawk on the Street, which broadcasts live from the New York Stock Exchange, is a daily business show that comes after the first 90 minutes of Wall Street trading.

Covering critical topics such as finance and economics, Morgan Brennan has over the course of her career at CNBC interviewed a lot of prominent people in the United States, including heads of state, corporate executives, and billionaires from different walks of life.

Meanwhile, Morgan once mentioned that transitioning from writing to television was quite hard for her. She said this was because she had to think on the spot rather than taking days or even weeks to formulate and get together her ideas.
