Ron Pederson is a Canadian actor, comedian, and theater director known for his extensive work in Canada and the United States.

When and where was he born?

What are some of his notable works?

He gained fame for his portrayal in The Beauty Queen of Leenane, for which he won a Sterling Award. He is known for his work on MADtv, The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, and for his role in the Stratford Festival’s The Merchant of Venice.

Has he received any awards for his work?

Yes, he has received several awards, including a Canadian Comedy Award for Best Male Improviser in 2011 and a Sterling Award for his portrayal in No Exit. He was also nominated for a Dora Award for Best Actor in a Musical for One Night Only.

What is The National Theatre of the World?

In 2008, he co-founded The National Theatre of the World with Matt Baram and Naomi Snieckus. This improv theater company has won three Canadian Comedy Awards and produced shows like Impromptu Splendor and The Carnegie Hall Show.

He joined the MainStage cast of Toronto’s Second City in 2023, further cementing his status as a prominent figure in the world of improv and comedy.
