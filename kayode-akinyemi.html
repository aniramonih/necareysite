Meet Kayode Akinyemi, Who Played Kaysan in “Vikings: Valhalla”

Kayode Akinyemi, aka Abdul Akinyemi (born on June 17, 1990), is a 32-year-old Austrian actor currently based in London, UK. He has worked on dozens of movies and shows, including “Max Justice,” “Dear Jesus,” “The Legend of Tarzan,” “White Powder,” and “The House Invictus.”

He was also in the movie “Decrypted” as Alonzo. It was a Tom Sands-directed project featuring artists like Sophia Myles, David Hoflin, Amanda Abbington, and Kevin McNally.

Kayode is currently earning attention because he appeared playing Kaysan in the highly anticipated season 2 of the Netflix series “Vikings: Valhalla,” which aired on January 12. In this adventure-packed action series, actors like Taylor James, Sam Corlett, Leo Suter, and Jóhannes Haukur Jóhannesson played lead characters.

According to IMDB, Akinyemi is currently working on his upcoming project, “Absent.” It is an Aysha Scott-directed movie, the story of which circulates around a mother trying to convince her partner to take responsibility for his son.

How Old is Kayode Akinyemi?

On June 17, 1990, Kayode Akinyemi (age 32) was born in Vienna, Austria. Sources say that the actor spent only a few years in Austria and came to Essex, England, when he was a toddler. Here he grew up under the full attention of his parents and got a quality education. First, he studied at Barking Abbey School Sixth Form and completed his schooling in 2009.

During his teen years, he developed an interest in acting and wanted to make a career as an actor, but because of the fear of cut-throat competition, he decided to get his college degree first. In 2009, Abdul joined the  University of Hertfordshire; here, he studied law and graduated with a Bachelor of Law degree in 2013.

Kayode Akinyemi’s Career

A year after graduating from college, Akinyemi decided to learn acting, so he joined the Identity School of Acting. He also attended the Kingdom School of Arts. While at Identity School, Kay began looking for opportunities to work as an actor. His search took him to the casting auditions of a Vince Gade movie, “Evermore.”

Luckily selectors liked him and picked him for the role of Samson. Meanwhile, he also landed the role of Wale in “Dear Jesus.” It was Danielle Scott-Haughton’s show, the story of which revolves around a young fashion journalist, Mercedes, played by Linda Adey.

In the coming years, he worked on numerous projects, including short movies like “2b or Not 2b in Love,” “Dead Bob,” “Collide: Soul Sisters,” “Rive,” and “Losing Pace.” He also played the role of a Mbolongo Warrior in “The Legend of Tarzan,” but it was uncredited, which is why many of you did not notice him.

Luckily in 2023, with “Vikings: Valhalla,” Akinyemi gained the deserved fame, and now hopefully, we will see him playing more prominent roles in the near future.
