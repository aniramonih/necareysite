Career

Chris Eubank Jr. began his career as the Nevada Amateur Golden Gloves Champion in the 165-pound weight class, and by his eighth amateur fight, he had become the Western States of the United States Amateur Golden Gloves Champion in his weight class. After going 1-1 in the 2008 National Golden Gloves, he finished his amateur career with a 24-2 record.

In 2011, he turned pro and signed with promoter Mick Hennessy, where he went 18-0 with 13 knockouts in three years. He agreed to appear on BoxNation on February 19, 2014, with his debut fight scheduled for February 22nd at York Hall in London. He knocked out Polish boxer Robert Swierzbinkski (13-2, 3 KOs) seven times before stopping him in round seven on May 10th at the Olympia in Liverpool.

Furthermore, on the undercard of Saunders vs. Blandamura on July 26th at the Phones 4u Arena in Manchester, he stopped Croatian journeyman Ivan Jukic (19-2, 12 KOs) after only 2 minutes and 40 seconds of the first round. His fight with Billy Joe Saunders was confirmed on October 3rd, but he lost his unbeaten record against Billy in a split decision. Frank Warren announced on December 30, 2014, that Chris would fight undefeated Russian boxer and WBA interim middleweight champion Dmitry Chudinov (14-0-2, 9 KOs) on the undercard of Tyson Fury vs. Christian Hammer on February 28, 2015, at The O2 Arena on London.
